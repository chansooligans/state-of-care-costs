# Federal transparency and cost policy scope

Date: 2026-05-03

## Product direction

Start as a "latest news" tool for people already informed about healthcare price transparency and cost policy. The news surface should be fast and current, but the legislation section should be more durable and educational.

## Capture universe

Track federal transparency and cost policy only for the first version.

Include:

- CMS Hospital Price Transparency and enforcement.
- Transparency in Coverage rules for commercial plans.
- No Surprises Act and broader Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2021 implementation.
- Gag Clause Prohibition Compliance Attestation.
- RxDC prescription drug and health care spending reporting.
- Inflation Reduction Act drug pricing implementation.
- 21st Century Cures Act and CMS interoperability/prior authorization rules where they affect access, cost, claims, directories, or authorization data.
- ERISA employer-plan fiduciary context when it connects to transparency, plan fees, claims data, or commercial coverage accountability.
- Federal site-neutral payment and other Medicare payment reforms when they are directly framed as cost policy.
- Active federal bills that would codify, expand, or materially change transparency/cost policy.

Exclude for now:

- State surprise billing laws, APCDs, state PDABs, state cost-growth benchmarks.
- General Medicare/Medicaid payment updates unless they relate to price transparency, patient cost, site of care, drug pricing, prior authorization, or commercial-market transparency.
- Hospital finance news without a clear transparency/cost-policy hook.
- Provider quality reporting unless it is paired with price, cost, shopping, or network information.

## Initial app lanes

- News and rulemaking signals.
- Legislation and statutory anchors.
- Fundamentals.
- Federal source watchlist.

## Source hierarchy

Prefer primary sources first:

- CMS Newsroom and CMS issue pages.
- Federal Register.
- Congress.gov.
- Regulations.gov when comment windows matter.
- DOL EBSA for ERISA and employer-plan materials.
- Treasury/HHS/DOL joint guidance for CAA and Transparency in Coverage.

Use journalism later for analysis, stakeholder reaction, and litigation context, but label it separately from primary-source policy movement.
